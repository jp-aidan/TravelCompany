3 Day Southwest of Ireland Tour
1,899

At 09.00 your expert driver-guide will collect you at your designated starting point in Dublin to start your 3-day tour of Ireland. You will have your driver-guide for 3 days while in the southwest of Ireland so please make sure to make the most of their knowledge and ask any questions while you are with them. Admission Charges and client lunch, dinners or accommodation are not included in this package. This tour is based on a 8 hour touring day, typically 09.00h to 17.00h following the tour outlined. The first and last day may take 9 to 10 hours depending on traffic conditions particularly in Dublin. Day1: Rock of Cashel | Blarney | Killarney Departing Dublin this morning our first stop will be at one of Ireland’s most historical sites the Rock of Cashel. This was once the seat of the High King of Ireland before it was transformed to a monastic settlement. It dates back over 900 years. From Cashel we will travel further south to the village of Blarney for some lunch. Here we have an opportunity to kiss the Blarney Stone and shop in one of Ireland’s best stores for Irish Gifts. From Blarney we will continue to Killarney to check into your accommodation for your dinner and overnight in Killarney. Day 2: Ring of Kerry Tour Today you will be taken on a full day tour of the Ring of Kerry taking in many of the beautiful scenic locations as well as ancient monuments and small attractions that the Ring of Kerry has to offer. Your tour will take you through Killorglin which annually elects a goat as king of its festival, Cahersiveen is a lively little town where the great liberator Daniel O’Connell was born. Waterville and Sneem also have many interesting stories and characters as we return towards Killarney through the Magillycuddy’s Reeks mountains for your dinner and overnight. Day 3: Cliffs of Moher |Burren | back to Dublin Today we will depart Killarney and say goodbye to Kerry as we head north into Co Clare, taking a small ferry (20 minutes) over the Shannon Estuary we arrive in Co Clare and make our way straight up to the now famous Cliffs of Moher. These imposing cliffs are 700 feet above sea level & offer unparalleled views over the Atlantic as they bear the full force of the Wild Atlantic Way. From the cliffs, we will stop in the small village of Doolin for lunch before taking in the ancient sites and scenery of the Burren. The Burren is a limestone region almost like a lunar landscape which is full of unique flora and fauna and many beautiful towns and villages. We will stop take the beautiful coast road overlooking Galway Bay, stopping at Corcomroe Abbey before we join the main road back to Dublin where you tour will end after a full 3 days. Check into your accommodation around 19.00hrs approx.
