Day1: Rock of Cashel | Blarney | Killarney

Departing Dublin this morning our first stop will be at one of Ireland’s most historical sites the Rock of Cashel. This was once the seat of the High King of Ireland before it was transformed to a monastic settlement. It dates back over 900 years. From Cashel we will travel further south to the village of Blarney for some lunch. Here we have an opportunity to kiss the Blarney Stone and shop in one of Ireland’s best stores for Irish Gifts. From Blarney we will continue to Killarney to check into your accommodation for your dinner and overnight in Killarney.

Day 2: Ring of Kerry Tour

Today you will be taken on a full day tour of the Ring of Kerry taking in many of the beautiful scenic locations as well as ancient monuments and small attractions that the Ring of Kerry has to offer. Your tour will take you through Killorglin which annually e lects a goat as king of its festival, Cahersiveen is a lively little town where the great liberator Daniel O’Connell was born. Waterville and Sneem also have many interesting stories and characters as we return towards Killarney through the Magillycuddy’s Reeks mountains for your dinner and overnight.

Day 3: Cliffs of Moher |Burren | back to Dublin

Today we will depart Killarney and say goodbye to Kerry as we head north into Co Clare, taking a small ferry (20 minutes) over the Shannon Estuary we arrive in Co Clare and make our way straight up to the now famous Cliffs of Moher. These imposing cliffs are 700 feet above sea level & offer unparalleled views over the Atlantic as they bear the full force of the Wild Atlantic Way. From the cliffs, we will stop in the small village of Doolin for lunch before taking in the ancient sites and scenery of the Burren. The Burren is a limestone region almost like a lunar landscape which is full of unique flora and fauna and many beautiful towns and villages. We will stop take the beautiful coast road overlooking Galway Bay, stopping at Corcomroe Abbey before we join the main road back to Dublin where you tour will end after a full 3 days. Check into your accommodation around 19.00hrs approx.

Day 3: Dingle Slea Head Tour

Like the Ring of Kerry, Dingle and the Slea Head is where Irish culture and scenery become one. Dingle itself is an Irish speaking town full of interesting shops, bars and a hive of activity in the summer months. The Slea Head is a scenic drive by Cliffside roads, ancient sites dot the landscape as we meander through the countryside stopping at the many points of interest. Returning through Dingle, enjoy lunch in the town before we make our way back to Killarney where a visit to Ross Castle or a Jaunting Car horse carriage ride through Killarney National Park might be a fun way to see Ireland in its natural beauty. Return to Killarney for your final dinner and overnight in the area.

Day 5: King Johns Castle | Killaloe | National Stud to Dublin

Our final day takes us into the beautiful Limerick City with a visit to King Johns Castle which dates all the way back to Viking times. Enjoy a full tour of this ancient Norman Castle before we continue up the Shannon River to stop in Killaloe for lunch overlooking the river. Driving onwards from there, we will make our way into Horseracing countryside to visit the only Stud farm visitor centre Ireland has in Co. Kildare. This is an amazing visit and portal in the world of horseracing. Visit the horse museum which tells the story of man’s relationship with horses. View the skeleton of Arkle, arguably the most successful racehorse in world history. See the Studs themselves, learn about their value and see the paddocks of these great horses, both current and former champions. Here you can also visit the millennium gardens and the 100-year-old Japanese Gardens. Departing the National Stud, we will continue to Dublin where you driver will point out some highlights as you make your way to your accommodation where your driver will bid you farewell and your Dublin city adventure begins.

Day 1: Dublin City Tour | Trinity College | The Book of Kells

Your tour begins at 09:00h at your designated pick-up point in Dublin. Head out for a panoramic tour of Dublin, seeing historic O’Connell Street, Georgian buildings and squares, and St Stephen’s Green. Stroll through Trinity College with a local guide and view one of Ireland’s greatest treasures: the Book of Kells, an illuminated manuscript crafted by monks in the 9th century. Return to your hotel and then enjoy an independent evening of dining and exploring as you wish.

Day 2: Sean's Bar | Galway Walking Tour

Head to the vibrant, artistic city of Galway. Along the way, enjoy a drink at Sean’s Bar in Athlone. The oldest pub in Europe, Sean’s Bar has been quenching thirsts since 900 AD. Join a local guide for a walking tour through Galway and discover this thriving city of medieval winding lanes and buzzing street life. Enjoy some free time to explore Galway on your own and dine in some of the magnificent bars and restaurants the city has to offer.

Day 3: Connemara | Catamaran Cruise | Kylemore Abbey & Gardens

Explore scenic Connemara to admire how constantly changing cloud formations give granite hills, sparkling lakes and peat bogs an added dimension. Take a short catamaran cruise of Killary Harbour, a 9-mile fjord. Head to Kylemore Abbey set beside a tranquil lake, and see its elegant rooms, the charming church and the Victorian Walled Gardens. Return to Galway where the evening is free to explore the city.

Day 4: Sheepdog Demonstration | Cliffs of Moher | Shannon Ferry | Killarney

Travel south around Galway Bay and through the stark beauty of the Burren, a distinctive limestone landscape. Immerse yourself in farm life as you watch trained sheepdogs herd their flock. Visit the Cliffs of Moher, rising 700 feet above the pounding Atlantic surf. Head to the friendly town of Killarney, taking a ferry across the River Shannon Estuary. In Killarney, take in an evening of traditional Irish entertainment, with a lively show of music and dance as you dine.

Day 5: Jaunting Car Ride | Ring of Kerry Highlights | Kenmare

Take a traditional horse-drawn jaunting car through Killarney National Park to scenic Ross Castle, set on the shores of Lough Leane. See the highlights of Ireland’s most popular scenic drive, the Ring of Kerry. The stunning coastal and mountain landscapes will provide plenty of photo opportunities. Stop in the Gap of Dunloe to visit Moriarty’s, a family business that has beautiful Irish gifts. Visit the colourful village of Kenmare and enjoy free time to explore its charming craft shops, art galleries and cafes. When you return to Killarney your evening is free to explore as you wish.

Day 6: Blarney Castle | Marine Bar in Dungarvan | House of Waterford Crystal

Drive through the mountains into rich farming country on the way to Blarney Castle. Kiss the famous “Stone of Eloquence” or explore the tranquil gardens. Travel along the coast to the Marine Bar in Dungarvan for some traditional music. Continue to Waterford, where you’ll watch skilled artisans create sparkling masterpieces during your visit to the House of Waterford Crystal.

Day 7: Waterford Walking Tour | Kilkenny | Dinner in Dublin

Join a local guide for a walking tour through the narrow streets of Waterford, discovering its fascinating past. Head to Kilkenny for free time to discover the medieval city centre. Tour the beautifully restored Kilkenny Castle to see glorious 17th-century tapestries, family portraits, and sumptuous furnishings reflecting the lifestyle of the Butler family who once resided there. Travel on to Dublin to check in to your accommodation and unfortunately your tour has come to an end.

Day 1: Dublin City Tour | Dublin Castle

Your tour begins at 09:00h at your designated Dublin pick up point. Take a panoramic tour of Dublin, seeing such sights as elegant Georgian townhouses, St. Stephen’s Green, and the River Liffey. Visit Dublin Castle, the seat of power and government in Ireland for many centuries. Take a guided tour through the castle and see the State Apartments, used today for official functions and presidential inaugurations. You are free to explore the Templebar area in the evening for dining and entertainment.

Day 2: Titanic Belfast | Belfast Walking Tour

Head north and tour central Belfast to see Victorian buildings, the imposing City Hall in Donegall Square, and the shipyards where RMS Titanic was built in 1912. Visit Titanic Belfast to learn about the ill-fated liner, which was built in the city. In the afternoon, join a local guide for a walking tour of Belfast before returning to Dublin to spend some time on your own.

Day 3: Tullamore Dew | Westport Walking Tour

Depart Dublin and drive through the midlands of Ireland. Visit Tullamore Dew and sample some of their world-renowned whiskey. Head west to Westport for some free time and a walking tour with a local guide. Check in to your accommodation and are free to enjoy the evening and try some of the bars that have traditional Irish music.

Day 4: Donegal | Triona Design Weaving Demonstratio

Travel through the rugged countryside of Donegal, renowned for heathery hills, sheep and the many tweeds made from their wool. Visit Triona Design in Ardara and enjoy a demonstration of handweaving. Return to Westport in the evening for free time.

Day 5: Sheepdog Demonstration | Westport House

Enjoy views of the pilgrimage mountain of Croagh Patrick and stop in the beautiful town of Leenane. Head to a local farm to watch skilled dogs herd sheep. View Killary Harbour, Ireland’s only fjord. Back in Westport, take a guided tour of Westport House, an 18th-century home complete with its original contents including portraits, furniture, silver, and porcelain. Spend the rest of the afternoon and evening enjoying the lively town of Westport at your leisure.

Day 6: Galway | Cliffs of Moher | Medieval Castle Banquet

Travel to Galway for some free time to explore the city. Head out to visit the majestic Cliffs of Moher, which soar 700 feet above the Atlantic. Enjoy an evening at a medieval-style banquet in an authentic Irish castle.

Day 7: John B. Keane’s Pub | Dingle Peninsula

Travel along the River Shannon into County Kerry. In Listowel, sample tea and scones in the family pub of the late, famed playwright John B. Keane. Journey along the Dingle Peninsula to Slea Head for glorious scenic views of hills and coastline. Stop in Dingle for some free time before heading to Killarney.

Day 8: Ring of Kerry Highlights | Kenmare | Jaunting Car Ride | Killarney

Travel along part of the Ring of Kerry to see spectacular mountain scenery at Moll’s Gap and Ladies’ View, on your way to Kenmare, where you’ll have lunch and some free time to explore. Visit Moriarty’s in the Gap of Dunloe. Take a scenic, traditional horse-drawn jaunting car to Ross Castle and the Kenmare Estate. Return to Killarney where your evening is free for independent dining and perhaps a visit to a local pub with live music.

Day 9: Blarney Castle | Marine Bar | Waterford Walking Tour

Visit Blarney Castle to climb the winding staircase and kiss the famed “Stone of Eloquence” or relax in the lovely gardens. Travel on to Dungarvan to enjoy an Irish coffee and traditional music at the Marine Bar. Continue to Waterford where a local guide will lead a stroll around the historic old port city.

Day 10: House of Waterford Crystal | Dunbrody Famine Ship | Taylors Three Rock

Witness the creation of crystal masterpieces at the House of Waterford Crystal. In New Ross explore the Dunbrody Famine Ship, which transported people to North America during the 1840’s famine. Return to Dublin to check in your accommodation (19:00h approx) and unfortunately your tour has come to an end.
